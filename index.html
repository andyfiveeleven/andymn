How I got here

My path is a bit weird, as you may have noticed, but the common thread is that I'm a builder, always have been, always will be. I thought I was destined to be a carpenter from a young age, but as I grew older, I figured out it's hard to change lives with tables and chairs. I moved to Seattle and traded in my wood shop for a tech office.

That first step into tech was through sales. I've always been a communicator and a people person, so sales was a natural place to go. I loved my time in sales, it was great getting on the ground working with users, and learning how someone sees a product for first time. As much as I liked it, something was missing, I didn't just want to just sell the product, I wanted to make it better. Simply put, I missed my time as a builder.

So here we are, I took that love of building and tinkering, mixed it with a need to change lives for the better, and topped it off with a bit of an obsession for the end user, and I got developer. I spend 5 months at Codefellows learning and itterating, allowing me to finally say, after all these years, that I'm a Full Stack Javascript developer.
